Mirror

Mirror is an established fashion brand that caters to the everyday clothing needs of children through 65 year olds. The design breif consisted of overhauling their brand identity and to help establish an online e-commerce presence to support their brick and mortar operations.

Interviews

Five in-person interviews were conducted. The age range was 22 to 28 with a gender break down of three females and two males. From these interviews I understood 5 things:

  • Online option overload is a barrier to purchasing.
  • Product filters are important.
  • Browsing online and purchasing offline is common.
  • “Would the clothing fit?”
  • Ease of purchasing and discounts are factors in brand loyalty.

Persona

Taking into account these findings I identified one core persona. I believe there are more, such as ‘the parent shopper’, but this wasn’t evident from my interviews.

Sarah is a mid twenties marketing manager, a few years into her career. She wants to look the part, is strapped for time and has a budget she likes to stick to.

Wireframes

The next step was to turn the user flow outlined above into a wireframe. The task is to purchase a shirt.

Branding & Color

Based on user research, 80% of the sample base stated that they really liked clean and minimalist interfaces.

The core colors chosen were navy blue and a complementary maroon which symbolize depth and tradition. I coupled these with shades of grey and used the core colors sparingly to enable the negative space and photography to stand out.

The typography selection of a sans-serif font (montserrat) was made to give a fresh feel to the website and break away from the traditonal feeling a serif font can give.

The message of these selections is that Mirror is an established brand that is in touch with the times. Mirror is showcasing to their existing consumer base that they’re a progressive retailer that is rooted in the fundamentals that made their brand strong.

User Testing

The hi-fidelity wireframes were linked together using InVision and tested on a new set of users.

User testing uncovered two issues—Design inconsistency’s regarding the “add to bag” page were noted by most and trouble with the design tool and the fact that only a certain user flow could be accessed.

The second was an issue of time, the first however could be rectified easily.

Reflection & Closing Comments

There are a few things I would improve with this case study:

For interviews I would like to first hand watch a users interaction with purchasing a product online.

For user testing, encouraging a higher level of commentary from the user, and asking probing questions about each page as the test progressed. Also some kind of warm-up activity or question to put the user at ease. I noticed that most seemed unsure and reserved in their actions and statements.

MindRock

MindRock was the final project of Le Wagon’s Full-stack Coding Bootcamp. The time frame was 10 days in total.

User Problem & Project Goals

In the ever growing and cut-throat job market that Chinese millennials face, confidence toward handling work place situations is their key to success, but many find it hard to define their brand of confidence. They understand it in theory but have trouble applying it, labeling themselves as introverted or ‘Zhai’.

The goal of this project was to use technology to give recent university graduates a tool to bridge the university to workplace skills gap.

Role & Scope

Our team consisted of four people. My role was product owner and User Experience design. Benjamin was our lead developer, Avery our backend developer and Claire was the frontend developer.

User Research

We spent the first day talking to a range of adults between the age of 23 and 28. The target users were all Chinese millennials.

About half were university students and the other half are actively seeking employment or currently in their first position after graduating university.

Our core research question was “How do you feel about the transition from being a student to a full-time employee?”

From this we learnt a few things:

  • Recent graduates feel like they don’t understand workplace norms.
  • The standard for professionally communicating and teamwork are poorly understood.
  • Some felt like they were stagnant or in a rut in terms of learning.

Wireframes

Prototype

In our solution we decided to tackle points two and three—Attempting to provide a solution that increases teamwork and breaks individuals out of their rut.

User Testing & Comments

MindRock gives users the option to complete a challenge in a team, and to share completed challenges with a friend, however, we realized from user testing that the hook to retain users is not sticky enough.

“I’ve got nothing to lose” said one user.

A possible solution could be negative contracts—for example, pledging a dollar amount at the beginning of a challenge and only receiving it back if they complete all the challenges—or a clearly defined goals system to incentivize the user to see a 7-day challenge through.

CloudSuite

User Problem & Project Goals

In China, there is no convenient digital platform to rent a jet for private use.

In a five day sprint, the goal of this project was to apply the Airbnb model to the private jet rental industry and to translate the experience into a WeChat Mini Program.

It was done as part of the Le Wagon full-stack coding bootcamp.

Roles & Tools

I was the front-end designer and developer. Claire was the lead developer, Avery, the PM and Benjamin was the backend developer.

We used Figma to collaboratively design the interface. Ruby on Rails for the backend and WeChat’s development environment for the frontend.

User Stories

We focused our time on building the following user stories:

  • As a user I want to be able to list my jet for others to rent.
  • As a user I want to be able to rent a jet.
  • As a user I want to be able to leave reviews.
  • As a user I want to be able to share a booking with someone else.

Sketches

Final Solution

We chose a navy blue and a deep red to symbolize strength and tradition. Feedback on this was positive and that these colors aligned with the idea of the Mini Program.

The dashboard page is designed to enable the user to navigate through viewing, listing and booking a jet as well as seeing their history. The idea here is convenience. The Mini Program is intended to aid the user in booking and minimize the time they spend in the app, as we have assumed they’re busy individuals with a high financial opportunity cost.

Project Results

The core objective was the adaption of the Airbnb model to WeChat Mini Programs, however given a longer time frame we would have liked to conduct real user interviews and user test our final solution.

A question raised when presenting CloudSuite was “Are users willing to undertake this kind of service on a mobile device?”. An interesting question that lay at the core of this model.

Some additional features in the product backlog are a map functionality, detailing where jets are located and an add-to-calendar function once a user has booked a jet.
